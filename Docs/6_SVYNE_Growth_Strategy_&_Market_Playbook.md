## 1. Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

Our sweet-spot clients are local businesses that fit the following criteria:

### Firmographics

- **Employee Count:** 2 to 20 people
  - Large enough to have coordination chaos
  - Small enough that the owner makes decisions instantly without a corporate board
- **Location:** Mobile County, Baldwin County, and surrounding Gulf Coast areas
- **Revenue:** $200K–$2M annually
  - Big enough to afford systems
  - Small enough to feel pain from inefficiency

### The Pain Profile

- **The Paper/Spreadsheet Trap:** They manage operations on dry-erase boards, carbon-copy paper books, text chains, or shared Excel files
- **Owner Dependency:** The owner is drowning in admin work and wants their time back
- **High Transaction Value:** A single missed lead or uncollected invoice costs them $500 to $5,000+
- **Growth Ambition:** They want to scale but are bottlenecked by manual processes

### Target Verticals (Priority Order)

| Priority | Vertical | Why First? | Typical Model |
| ---------- | ---------- | ----------- | --------------- |
| 1 | Event Venues & Artists | Proven (L&A Studios), low friction, transaction model | Model A |
| 2 | Auto Collision Shops | High ticket value, scheduling pain, local density | Model B |
| 3 | Trade Contractors (HVAC, Electrical, Plumbing) | Dispatch chaos, invoicing delays, mobile workforce | Model B |
| 4 | Salons & Spas | Appointment-based, repeat customers, no-shows | Model B |
| 5 | Restaurants & Cafes | Reservation management, staff scheduling, inventory | Hybrid |
| 6 | Professional Services (Law, Accounting, Consulting) | Lead capture, appointment scheduling, billing | Model B |
| 7 | Retail & Specialty Shops | Inventory, customer loyalty, multi-channel sales | Model B |

### Who We DON'T Target (Yet)

- Enterprise (>50 employees) — too complex for solo developer
- Pure e-commerce — Shopify/Square already own this
- SaaS startups — not our market
- Businesses with dedicated IT staff — they build in-house

## 2. Market Entry Strategy

### Phase 1: The Free 15-Minute Operational Flow Audit

### The Hook

Instead of selling software, offer a Free Operational Flow Audit.

### How it works

1. Walk in during off-peak hours (or call/message for remote businesses)
2. Offer 15 minutes to map their current "Inputs, Systems, and Outputs"
3. Draw their workflow on paper
4. Highlight manual steps, double-entries, and communication gaps in red
5. Show how Svyne connects these into one automated dashboard

### Why it works

- Feels like consultative business advice, not a sales pitch
- Creates immediate visual contrast (messy paper vs. clean system)
- Owner sees their own business, not a generic demo
- Positions you as expert, not vendor

### The Script

> "I help local businesses save 5-10 hours a week by replacing their manual paperwork with a simple system. I'm not selling anything today — I just want to understand how you currently handle [leads/scheduling/invoicing]. Can I sketch out your workflow for 15 minutes? I'll show you exactly where time and money are leaking."

### Phase 2: The 3-Day Offline Interactive Mockup

### The Bridge

Turn skepticism into trust by showing, not telling.

### How it works (2)

1. Spend 2–3 days configuring a personalized frontend mockup
2. Use their actual logo, colors, and workflow steps
3. Run it offline on your tablet or laptop
4. Meet again and let them click through their own dashboard

### The Presentation

> "I built a mockup of your custom operations board. This isn't generic software — it's configured specifically for how you run [Business Name]. See how [specific feature] eliminates the [specific pain point] you mentioned?"

### Why it works (2)

- Tangible proof you can deliver
- Emotional connection to their own brand
- Removes "software is complicated" objection
- Creates sunk-cost bias (you already invested time)

### Phase 3: The Risk-Free Case Study Offer

### The Close

Lower upfront friction to secure your first 3 portfolio builders.

### The Deal

- Setup fee: $250 – $500 (covers deployment and API costs)
- Monthly: $99/mo
- No long-term contract

### In Exchange

- Video testimonial (2-3 minutes)
- Written Google review
- Introduction to 2 other local business owners
- Permission to use their business as a case study

### Why it works (3)

- Almost no risk for client
- You get proof for future sales
- Referrals in tight-knit community
- Portfolio material for website

### Hard Cutoff Rule

After 3 case studies, full pricing resumes. No exceptions. This prevents the "forever discount" trap.

### Phase 4: Vertical Niche Domination

### The Pivot

Once you have 3 case studies in one vertical, package it into a repeatable playbook.

### How it works (3)

1. Identify your most successful vertical (e.g., Auto Collision)
2. Document the exact configuration: modules, workflows, integrations
3. Create industry-specific marketing:
   - "How [Auto Shop Name] Reduced Scheduling Conflicts by 90%"
   - Before/after screenshots
   - ROI calculation
4. Target similar businesses within 50 miles
5. Use case studies as social proof in outbound outreach

### Why it works (4)

- Reduces sales cycle (they see their peer's success)
- Reduces build time (copy-paste configuration)
- Establishes you as THE expert for that vertical
- Creates word-of-mouth within industry associations

## 4. B2B Channel Partnerships: The Agency Lever

### The Mutual Win-Win

### Their Gap

Small marketing agencies (1-5 employees) can design beautiful websites and run Facebook Ads, but they have zero database integration or software development skills. They miss out on high-ticket jobs because they can't build custom client portals, SMS routing, or invoicing integrations.

### Your Value

You act as their On-Demand Technical Partner. They sell the project and manage the client; you configure the Svyne modular systems behind the scenes.

### The Deal (2)

- Agency marks up Svyne services 30-50%
- You handle all technical work
- White-label option available (Svyne branding hidden)
- Revenue share: 70% you, 30% agency (negotiable)

### How to Find Agency Partners

1. Search locally for:
   - "Web Design Saraland"
   - "Mobile AL Marketing Agency"
   - "Social Media Management Baldwin County"
   - "SEO Mobile Alabama"

2. Audit their offerings:
   - If limited to "brand sites" or social ads = prime candidate
   - If they already offer "custom development" = less need for you

3. The Pitch:

   > "I build custom operational systems for local businesses — CRMs, scheduling, invoicing, text automation. Most of your clients probably need this but you don't offer it. I can be your technical backend. You sell it, I build it, we both make money."

4. The Proof:
   - Show your case studies
   - Offer to build ONE free demo for their best client prospect
   - Let them see the quality before committing

### Ideal Agency Partner Profile

- 1-5 employees
- Local client base (not national)
- Primarily design/marketing focused
- No in-house developers
- Owner-operated (fast decisions)
- Active in local business community

## 5. Client Qualification Checklist

Use this BEFORE investing time in a demo:

### Must-Haves (All Required)

- [ ] 2-20 employees
- [ ] Currently using paper, spreadsheets, or whiteboards for core operations
- [ ] Owner is the decision-maker (or can approve $1,500+ spend)
- [ ] Business has been operating 1+ years (not a startup)
- [ ] At least 10 Google reviews (proves real traction)

### Strong Indicators (2+ Preferred)

- [ ] Actively answering phones/social messages (cares about lead capture)
- [ ] Obvious communication gap (no online booking, manual invoicing)
- [ ] High-value bottleneck (missing one lead = $500+ loss)
- [ ] Owner expressed frustration with current tools
- [ ] Recently tried software and quit (aware of the problem)

### Red Flags (Disqualify)

- [ ] "We don't need technology" (unaware of problem)
- [ ] "Just send me a brochure" (not engaged)
- [ ] Can't articulate their biggest operational pain
- [ ] Looking for "cheap" not "valuable"
- [ ] No time for a 15-minute audit (too busy or not serious)

### Scoring

- 5/5 Must-Haves + 3+ Strong Indicators = **Hot Lead** (prioritize)
- 4/5 Must-Haves + 2 Strong Indicators = **Warm Lead** (nurture)
- <4 Must-Haves or any Red Flag = **Disqualify** (move on)

## 6. Competitive Positioning

### How to Talk About Competitors (Without Sounding Defensive)

**The Rule:** Never bash competitors. Position them correctly, then show where Svyne wins.

### Eventbrite

### When they win

- National events with massive scale
- Built-in marketing and discovery
- Complex multi-day festivals
- International attendees

### When Svyne wins

- Local venues (<500 capacity)
- Data ownership (Eventbrite keeps attendee emails)
- Custom seating charts (Eventbrite doesn't do visual seating)
- Lower total fees at local scale
- Personal support (you answer the phone)
- Widget embedding on client's own website

### The Script (2)

> "Eventbrite is great if you're running a 5,000-person festival and need national marketing. But if you're a local venue and want to own your customer list, keep people on your own website, and have someone local who can adjust your seating chart at 10 PM the night before the event — that's where we come in."

### Jobber / ServiceTitan

### When they win (2)

- Large contractor teams (20+ field staff)
- Enterprise features (GPS tracking, complex routing)
- National franchises

### When Svyne wins (2)

- Local shops (2-10 employees)
- Custom workflows (not forced into their template)
- No long-term contract
- Data ownership
- Local support
- Lower monthly cost

### The Script (3)

> "Jobber is powerful if you have 30 technicians across three states. But if you're a local shop with 4 employees and you need a system that matches YOUR workflow — not the other way around — we build exactly what you need without the enterprise price tag."

### Square / Shopify

### When they win (3)

- E-commerce and retail POS
- Simple product sales
- Online stores

### When Svyne wins (3)

- Service businesses (not product sales)
- Appointment-based operations
- Complex workflows (not just checkout)
- B2B relationships
- Custom invoicing and estimates

### The Script (4)

> "Square is perfect if you're selling t-shirts online. But if you're a contractor who needs to dispatch crews, track job progress, and send text updates to customers — that's a completely different problem. We solve the operations side, not the checkout side."

### DIY / Spreadsheets

### When they win (4)

- Single-person businesses
- Extremely simple operations
- No growth plans

### When Svyne wins (4)

- Any business with 2+ employees
- Anyone who has lost a lead or double-booked
- Anyone who wants to grow without drowning in admin

### The Script (5)

> "Spreadsheets work until they don't. The question is: how many hours are you spending on manual work that could be automated? And how many customers have you lost because something fell through the cracks?"

## 7. Success Metrics & Milestones

- [ ] Audits completed
- [ ] Mockups presented
- [ ] Proposals sent
- [ ] New clients signed
- [ ] Hours spent building vs. selling

### Monthly Metrics (Review First of Month)

- [ ] Total monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
- [ ] New clients acquired

- [ ] Revenue vs. target
- [ ] Client count vs. target
- [ ] Vertical mix (are you diversified?)

- [ ] Module library growth (new modules built)
